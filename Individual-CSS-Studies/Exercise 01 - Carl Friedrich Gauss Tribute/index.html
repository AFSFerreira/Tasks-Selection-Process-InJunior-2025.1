Carl Friedrich Gauss

The Eternal Prince of Mathematics

About his History...

Born in Brunswick, Germany, in 1777, Gauss was a descendant of a humble family. His notable achievements have been recognized since his childhood, when an apocryphal account mentions that he was able to identify a calculation error in his father's tax accounting, clearly demonstrating his mathematical aptitude.

With the participation of his teachers and the help of his devoted mother, at the age of 14 he received financial support to pay for his mathematics studies at the University of Göttingen, where he could begin to make his first significant contributions to the scientific community. It's first notable discovery was the realization that it was possible to construct a regular polygon with 17 sides using only basic drawing instruments, namely a ruler and compass. The steps involved in this feat, which at first glance seemed quite unimpressive, would later inspire the author responsible for creating one of the most fascinating fields of study in mathematics: Galois Theory.

“ It is not the knowledge but the act of learning, not the possession but the act of getting there, that gives the greatest satisfaction. ”

Interesting facts about Gauss:

  • He once impressed his teacher by quickly adding all the numbers from 1 to 100;
  • He was one of the first to formulate a conjecture about the distribution of prime numbers, known as Gauss's Law for the Distribution of Prime Numbers.
  • Gauss did not limit himself to mathematics; he made significant contributions in astronomy, geodesy, physics and statistics, being one of the most versatile scientists of all time.
